# Lesson 30: Wanting and intending (muốn, định)

To talk about the future, you differentiate between what you *desire* to do and what you *plan* to do.

### Desire with "muốn"

**Muốn** expresses a general wish or want.

<pre>
Tôi <b>muốn</b> uống cà phê.
"I <b>want</b> to drink coffee."
</pre>

<pre>
Em <b>muốn</b> mua cái áo này.
"I <b>want</b> to buy this shirt."
</pre>

### Planning with "định"

**Định** implies active intention or a schedule. It means "plan to" or "intend to."

<pre>
Cuối tuần này tôi <b>định</b> về quê.
"This weekend I <b>plan</b> to return to (my) hometown."
</pre>

<pre>
Anh ấy <b>định</b> mua nhà mới.
"He <b>intends</b> to buy a new house."
</pre>

### Polite Wanting with "thích"

Often, Vietnamese speakers use **thích** (like) to express a preference, which functions as a soft request.

<pre>
Bạn có <b>thích</b> đi xem phim không?
"Do you <b>like</b> (want) to go see a movie?"
</pre>

### Internal vs. External Plan

**Muốn** is a feeling inside you. **Định** is a decision you have made.

<pre>
Hè này bạn <b>định</b> đi đâu?
"Where do you <b>plan</b> to go this summer?"
</pre>

### Examples

<pre>
Chúng tôi không <b>muốn</b> làm phiền.
"We don't <b>want</b> to bother (you)."
</pre>

<pre>
Tôi <b>định</b> học tiếng Việt.
"I <b>intend</b> to study Vietnamese."
</pre>

### Note on nuance

You can **muốn** many things (like winning the lottery) without ever **định** to do anything about them. Reserve **định** for things you are actually going to do.
