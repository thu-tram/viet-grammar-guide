# Lesson 36: Lists and enumeration

When you want to list several items or steps in a row, Vietnamese keeps it simple. You can use pauses (commas) or specific marker words to organize your thoughts.

### Simple Lists

Just like in English, you can list items one after another. In Vietnamese, it's very common to only use **và** (and) between the last two items, or even skip it entirely if the context is clear.

<pre>
Trong giỏ có táo, cam <b>và</b> xoài.
“In the basket, there are apples, oranges, <b>and</b> mangoes.”
</pre>

### Ordered Steps (**thứ nhất, thứ hai...**)

To count items in a list (like "firstly, secondly"), use **thứ** followed by the number.

- **Thứ nhất**: Firstly / First
- **Thứ hai**: Secondly / Second
- **Thứ ba**: Thirdly / Third

<pre>
<b>Thứ nhất</b> là đi chợ, <b>thứ hai</b> là nấu cơm.
“<b>First</b> is going to the market, <b>second</b> is cooking rice.”
</pre>

### The "Also" Link (**với lại**)

In spoken Vietnamese, when adding one more thing to a list, people often use **với lại** or **còn** (and also).

<pre>
Tôi thích ăn phở, <b>với lại</b> cả bánh mì nữa.
“I like eating pho, <b>and also</b> banh mi too.”
</pre>

### Examples

<pre>
Bạn cần mang theo hộ chiếu, vé máy bay <b>và</b> tiền.
“You need to bring your passport, flight ticket, <b>and</b> money.”
</pre>

<pre>
Có ba lý do. <b>Thứ nhất</b>, nó rẻ. <b>Thứ hai</b>, nó đẹp.
“There are three reasons. <b>First</b>, it’s cheap. <b>Second</b>, it’s beautiful.”
</pre>

<pre>
Em thích màu xanh, màu đỏ <b>với</b> màu vàng.
“I like blue, red, <b>and</b> yellow.”
</pre>

<pre>
Cần mua sữa, trứng, <b>còn</b> rau thì nhà có rồi.
“(I) need to buy milk, eggs; <b>as for</b> vegetables, we already have them.”
</pre>

### Note on nuance

Notice how **với** (with) is often used as "and" in lists of objects. When enumerating points in a conversation, you'll hear people use their fingers or just say "Một là... hai là..." (One is... two is...). This is a very common way to keep a story or a list of tasks organized.
