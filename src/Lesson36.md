# Lesson 36: Lists and enumeration

Listing items or steps in Vietnamese relies on rhythm and a few specific markers.

### Rhythmic Listing

Often, simple commas (pauses) are enough. The word **và** (and) usually only appears before the final item, if at all.

<pre>
Táo, cam <b>và</b> xoài.
"Apples, oranges, <b>and</b> mangoes."
</pre>

### Ordering with "thứ"

To rank items ("firstly, secondly"), use **thứ** + number.

<pre>
<b>Thứ nhất</b> là rẻ.
"<b>First</b> is (it's) cheap."
</pre>

<pre>
<b>Thứ hai</b> là đẹp.
"<b>Second</b> is (it's) beautiful."
</pre>

### Conversational Additions

To add "oh, and also" to a list, use **với lại** or **còn**.

<pre>
Tôi thích phở, <b>với lại</b> bánh mì.
"I like pho, <b>and (also)</b> banh mi."
</pre>

### The Final "với"

In spoken lists, **với** (with) often replaces **và** as a connector.

<pre>
Xanh, đỏ <b>với</b> vàng.
"Blue, red, <b>with (and)</b> yellow."
</pre>

### Examples

<pre>
Cần mua sữa, trứng <b>với</b> rau.
"Need to buy milk, eggs, <b>and</b> vegetables."
</pre>

### Note on nuance

**Thứ nhất... thứ hai...** is great for organizing an argument or explaining reasons. **Với lại** is very casual, used when you remember one more thing to add to the pile.
